

LEFT TRIANGULAR LIGAMENT


The left triangular ligament is a large peritoneal fold. 

It connects the posterior part of the upper surface of the left lobe of the liver to the thoracic diaphragm. 


== Structure ==

The left triangular ligament connects the posterior part of the upper surface of the left lobe of the liver to the thoracic diaphragm. 

Its anterior layer is continuous with the left layer of the falciform ligament.
